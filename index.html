Expansion vs. acquisition — key differences

Acquisition sequences lead with pain the prospect hasn't solved yet. Expansion sequences lead with value the customer has already experienced and show them the next level. The tone shifts from "you have a problem" to "you've earned more." Both motions use the same personas (Olivia, Peter, Charlie, Sam) but the narrative arc is fundamentally different. Marty (Marketing/Elite Expansion) is an expansion-only persona who enters at the Marketing Suite and Elite upgrade conversation. Same engines, different last mile: 6sense, Salesforce, and Marketo are the shared foundation for both motions. The only difference is where the rep engages — Outreach for AEs (acquisition), Qualified + Slack for AMs (expansion).

"How can we use our top customers' relationships and reputation to get in front of other contractors like them? All these contractors work together and know each other."

This is a distinct acquisition motion that sits alongside Ghost Tier. Ghost Tier uses inbound trial signals to find accounts. Network Expansion uses existing customer relationships to find accounts. Both feed the same TAL → tier → sequence architecture.
